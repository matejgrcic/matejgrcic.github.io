I am Matej Grcić, PhD in Machine Learning and research associate at [Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computing](https://www.fer.unizg.hr/en).
My research focuses on combining the expressiveness of deep learning and versatility of probabilistic modeling to develop robust and reliable ML models for real-world applications.

During my PhD studies, under supervision of [prof. Siniša Šegvić](http://www.zemris.fer.hr/~ssegvic/index_en.html),
my research focused on enhancing the safey of deep models deployed in the real world.
Specifically, I worked on [open-set](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/10496197) 
and [outlier-aware](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2023W/VAND/papers/Grcic_On_Advantages_of_Mask-Level_Recognition_for_Outlier-Aware_Segmentation_CVPRW_2023_paper.pdf) 
semantic segmentation with a strong emphasis on
real-time applications such as [autonomous driving](https://youtu.be/4Ggcn8mOQdE).
I also worked on semantic segmentation of traffic scenes with adverse driving conditions, 
which resulted in winning [CVPR'22 Vision4AllSeasons](https://vision4allseason.net/workshop-at-cvpr22) competition.

I spent three semesters at [EPFL](https://www.epfl.ch/en/) as visiting PhD student supervised by [prof. Maria Brbic](https://people.epfl.ch/maria.brbic) and funded by [Swiss Government Excellence Scholarship](https://www.sbfi.admin.ch/sbfi/en/home/education/scholarships-and-grants/swiss-government-excellence-scholarships.html).
There, I explored fundamental machine learning topics, including recognition and representation learning under limited supervision.
This research has led to the development of weakly-supervised models capable of [discovering fine-grained subclasses](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11070).


Prior to PhD, I earned both Master's and Bachelor's degrees from the University of Zagreb, where I was honored with the Rector's Award for outstanding individual research.
During my studies, I also gained valuable industry experience through internships at [LifeNome](https://lifenomeapi.com), [Axilis](https://www.axilis.com/), and [CROZ](https://croz.net).

I am always open to discussions and collaborations. Feel free to reach out to me via email.
